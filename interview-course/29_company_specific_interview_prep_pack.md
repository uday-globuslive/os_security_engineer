# Module 29: Company-Specific Interview Prep Pack

## Purpose
Adapt your answers to different interview styles without changing technical truth.

## Universal strategy
For every company:
- Keep one technical core answer.
- Tune emphasis: depth, scale, leadership, or principles.
- Always include measurable outcomes.

## Microsoft-style pattern
Typical focus:
- Collaboration and cross-team execution.
- Practical engineering impact.
- Security + reliability + developer experience balance.

Answer style:
- Explain architecture clearly.
- Show ownership and partnering behavior.
- Include improvement you institutionalized.

Sample framing:
"I partnered with platform and application teams to deploy a non-root, multi-stage baseline and integrated scan gates in CI, reducing critical CVEs while keeping release flow stable."

## Amazon-style pattern
Typical focus:
- Leadership principles and decision ownership.
- Metrics, urgency, and deep dives.
- High-bar incident and operational discipline.

Answer style:
- Start with customer/business impact.
- Show data-driven decision and bias for action.
- Show long-term mechanism built after fix.

Sample framing:
"I reduced critical exposure within the SLA and then built an automated base-image refresh mechanism to prevent recurrence across services."

## Google-style pattern
Typical focus:
- Systems thinking and scale.
- Technical rigor and abstraction.
- Clear design tradeoff reasoning.

Answer style:
- Define constraints first.
- Compare multiple approaches.
- Choose one and justify with scale/cost/risk.

Sample framing:
"Given 300 services and mixed runtimes, I proposed a centralized image factory with policy-as-code and signed artifacts, trading some team flexibility for consistent risk controls."

## Meta-style pattern
Typical focus:
- Speed with technical depth.
- Troubleshooting strength.
- Impact at scale and iteration pace.

Answer style:
- Crisp problem diagnosis.
- Rapid mitigation and follow-up hardening.
- Metrics and iterative improvements.

## Startup/Scale-up pattern
Typical focus:
- Hands-on versatility.
- Pragmatic controls under constraints.
- Fast delivery with minimal overhead.

Answer style:
- Explain lean controls that matter most.
- Prioritize top risk first.
- Show progressive maturity roadmap.

## Question adaptation matrix
1. "Tell me about a critical CVE you fixed"
- Microsoft: collaboration + platformization.
- Amazon: urgency, ownership, metrics.
- Google: approach alternatives and scale model.
- Startup: practical and fast mitigation.

2. "How do you reduce attack surface?"
- Microsoft: secure defaults in templates.
- Amazon: measurable reduction and audit cadence.
- Google: architecture tradeoffs and reproducibility.
- Startup: highest-value controls first.

3. "Why do HIGH CVEs remain after patching?"
- Microsoft: educate teams with clear triage process.
- Amazon: root-cause mechanism and prevention.
- Google: data-source and matching model explanation.
- Startup: quick diagnosis playbook.

## Behavioral translation guide
Convert same story per style:
- Collaborative tone for Microsoft.
- Principle-and-ownership tone for Amazon.
- Design-and-tradeoff tone for Google.
- Execution-and-speed tone for startups.

## Final practice routine
- Pick 5 questions.
- Answer each in 2 variants:
1. Deep technical variant.
2. Company-style variant.

Goal: remain authentic while matching interview expectations.

# Module 14: Interview Masterclass

## 1) How Interviewers Evaluate You
They evaluate four dimensions:
- Technical depth.
- Practical ownership.
- Risk judgment.
- Communication clarity.

## 2) Answer Framework (90-Second Model)
Use this structure:
1. Context.
2. Problem and risk.
3. Technical actions.
4. Validation.
5. Measurable result.

## 3) Top 20 Must-Practice Questions
1. Tell me about a critical CVE you remediated.
2. Why do HIGH CVEs remain after patching?
3. How do you reduce attack surface in images?
4. Explain your triage model for vulnerabilities.
5. How do you choose base images?
6. Trivy vs Grype output differs. What do you do?
7. How do you handle no-fix-yet CVEs?
8. How do you enforce policy gates in CI/CD?
9. What does a good exception process include?
10. How do you prevent regressions while patching quickly?
11. What is SBOM and how do you use it?
12. Why sign images?
13. Explain image tag vs digest security implications.
14. How do you prove a vulnerability fix worked?
15. How do you prioritize CVEs across hundreds of images?
16. How do you reduce image size and maintain functionality?
17. What runtime controls do you enforce in Kubernetes?
18. How do you report security posture to leadership?
19. Describe a security incident you helped prevent.
20. What would you improve in our current process first?

## 4) Red Flags to Avoid in Interviews
- Speaking only in tool names, no actual workflow.
- No measurable outcomes.
- No mention of tradeoffs.
- No validation or rollback strategy.

## 5) Mock Round Format
Round 1 (30 min): fundamentals and tooling.
Round 2 (45 min): scenario and design deep dive.
Round 3 (30 min): behavioral and ownership.

## 6) Final Interview-Day Checklist
- 5 STAR stories memorized in bullet form.
- 10 command-level examples ready.
- 3 architecture explanations ready.
- 1-page personal cheat sheet reviewed.
